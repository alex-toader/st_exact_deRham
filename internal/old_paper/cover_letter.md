I am submitting the manuscript entitled "Exactness-preserving discrete de Rham complexes under Bloch-periodic boundary conditions" for consideration in ESAIM: Mathematical Modelling and Numerical Analysis.

The paper shows that the standard construction of discrete exterior calculus (DEC) operators breaks the de Rham exact sequence under Bloch-periodic boundary conditions on unstructured polyhedral meshes. This failure is structural, not numerical: no per-edge phase assignment can restore exactness. We construct an alternative curl operator via a face-boundary recurrence that preserves exactness, prove uniqueness and canonicity of the resulting curl–curl operator, and compute its gauge kernel dimension via twisted de Rham cohomology. Three structural consequences of inexactness are identified: the pollution count equals the rank excess of d₁ (an algebraic identity), the Hodge decomposition degrades to random-baseline hybridization, and the eigenvalue trace is conserved. On periodic Voronoi tessellations, the exact complex yields isotropic dispersion. All results are verified by a reproducible test suite of 57 tests across 7 files.

I believe the work is appropriate for ESAIM: M2AN because it addresses structure-preserving discretization of Maxwell eigenvalue problems on polyhedral meshes, connecting discrete exterior calculus with finite element exterior calculus (FEEC) and twisted cohomology with local coefficients.
